Learn More: Climate Change and Green Energy

Climate change can feel abstract until it touches something you love — your home, your food, your weekends without floods or heat warnings. This page turns the big picture into something concrete: what’s changing, what green energy really is, and why shifting now matters for your life and your future.

What Climate Change Is

  • Definition: Long-term changes in temperature, rainfall, and weather patterns over decades, not daily weather.
  • Greenhouse effect: Gases like CO₂, methane, and nitrous oxide trap heat, keeping Earth habitable — but excess gases intensify this effect.
  • Main drivers: Burning coal, oil, and gas for electricity, transport, and industry; deforestation; and agriculture that releases methane and nitrous oxide.
  • What’s different now: The speed and scale. Warming of about 1.1°C since the late 1800s is happening far faster than natural cycles.
Sources: IPCC AR6; NASA Climate Change

What We’re Already Seeing

  • Heat and extremes: Longer, hotter heatwaves; storms carrying more rain; longer droughts.
  • Rising seas: Warmer oceans and melting ice push tides higher, worsening storm surges.
  • Ecosystem stress: Coral bleaching, shifting fish stocks, forest pests, and biodiversity loss.
  • Health impacts: Heat stress, smog, and expanding diseases.
  • Economic costs: Damaged homes, disrupted work, higher food and energy prices.
Sources: IPCC AR6; NOAA; WHO Climate and Health

Why This Matters in the Philippines

  • Climate risk: The Philippines is one of the most climate-vulnerable countries, facing frequent typhoons and floods.
  • Energy reality: Coal provides much of the electricity, linking power prices and air quality to fossil fuels.
  • Opportunity window: The country has strong renewable potential — solar, geothermal, wind, and hydro.
Sources: Germanwatch CRI; DOE Philippines; PAGASA

What Green Energy Is

  • Solar: Converts sunlight into electricity.
  • Wind: Turbines harness moving air for power.
  • Hydropower: Flowing water spins turbines.
  • Geothermal: Taps underground heat for steady energy.
  • Biomass: Uses organic waste for energy.
Sources: IRENA; DOE Philippines

Why Green Energy Matters Now

  • Cuts emissions: Replaces fossil fuels and reduces pollution.
  • Cleans the air: Reduces smog and related diseases.
  • Improves energy security: Uses local, renewable sources.
  • Creates jobs: Boosts green industries and local work.
  • Builds resilience: Microgrids and storage strengthen disaster response.
Sources: IEA; UN SDG 7; WHO

Key Takeaways

  • Root cause: Greenhouse gases from fossil fuels.
  • Real impacts: Stronger storms, rising seas, higher costs.
  • Solutions: Efficiency, electrification, renewables, storage.
  • Why it matters: Cleaner air, lower bills, and a safer future.
